## Interview Purpose
Validate the "Robert Chen" building manager persona and understand current maintenance workflows, pain points, and needs.

## Target Participants
- 2-3 building/property managers
- Managing residential buildings (apartments, condos)
- Experience with maintenance request processes

## Interview Structure (30-45 minutes)

### Part 1: Introduction (5 minutes)
- Thank participant for their time
- Explain purpose: "We're developing a building maintenance app and want to understand your current process"
- Assure confidentiality: "All responses are anonymous and for research only"
- Get permission to take notes/record (if applicable)

### Part 2: Current Process (15 minutes)

**Opening Question:**
"Can you walk me through what happens when a tenant reports a maintenance issue?"

**Follow-up Questions:**
1. **Communication Channels:**
   - How do tenants typically report issues? (Phone, email, in-person, other?)
   - What percentage use each method?
   - Do you have preferred channels?

2. **Issue Tracking:**
   - How do you track maintenance requests currently? (Spreadsheet, software, paper, other?)
   - Show me your current tracking system if possible
   - How do you prioritize issues? (Urgent vs. non-urgent)

3. **Assignment & Coordination:**
   - How do you assign work to maintenance staff?
   - How do maintenance staff update you on progress?
   - How do you communicate status back to tenants?

4. **Documentation:**
   - What information do you document for each repair?
   - How do you track costs, time spent, parts used?
   - Where is this information stored?

### Part 3: Pain Points & Challenges (10 minutes)

**Key Questions:**
1. "What's the most frustrating part of your current maintenance process?"
2. "Where do things most often go wrong or get delayed?"
3. "What information do you wish you had but don't?"
4. "How do you handle emergency/urgent issues differently?"
5. "What's your biggest communication challenge with tenants? With maintenance staff?"

**Specific Pain Points to Explore:**
- Lost/misplaced work orders
- Lack of status visibility
- Tenant complaints about slow response
- Difficulty tracking costs
- Paperwork burden
- Language/communication barriers

### Part 4: Ideal Solution (10 minutes)

**Scenario-Based Questions:**
1. "Imagine your ideal maintenance system. What would it do for you?"
2. "What information would you want to see on your main dashboard?"
3. "How would you want to receive alerts/notifications?"
4. "What reporting capabilities would be most valuable?"

**Feature Prioritization:**
Show quick mockup or describe potential features:
- "How valuable would real-time status tracking be?"
- "Would photo documentation from tenants help?"
- "How about automated notifications to tenants?"
- "What about cost tracking and reporting?"

### Part 5: Demographics & Wrap-up (5 minutes)

**Background Information:**
- How many buildings do you manage?
- How many units total?
- How many maintenance staff?
- Years of experience?
- Current software/tools used?

**Closing:**
1. "Is there anything about maintenance management we haven't discussed?"
2. "Would you be open to testing a prototype in the future?"
3. Thank participant, provide contact information
4. Offer to share findings if interested

## Interview Script Template

**Introduction Script:**
"Hi [Name], thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. My name is [Your Name] and I'm working on a building maintenance app designed to streamline repair requests and tracking. I'd like to understand your current process and challenges to make sure we build something that actually helps people in your role. This should take about 30-45 minutes. Is now still a good time?"

**Transition Scripts:**
- "Now I'd like to understand how maintenance requests flow through your system..."
- "Let's talk about some of the challenges you might face..."
- "If you could design the perfect system, what would it look like?"

**Closing Script:**
"Thank you so much for your insights. This has been incredibly helpful. We'll be using this information to design our app. Would you be interested in seeing what we build or testing a prototype in the future?"

## Key Insights to Capture

### Process Mapping:
- Current workflow steps
- Handoff points between people/systems
- Communication touchpoints
- Approval/decision points

### Pain Points:
- Specific bottlenecks
- Communication breakdowns
- Data gaps
- Time-consuming tasks

### Needs & Wishes:
- Information needs at different stages
- Reporting requirements
- Integration needs with other systems
- Mobile vs. desktop preferences

### Success Metrics:
- How they measure maintenance effectiveness
- What building owners care about
- Tenant satisfaction indicators
- Cost control measures

## Next Steps After Interviews

1. **Transcribe notes** within 24 hours
2. **Identify patterns** across interviews
3. **Update personas** based on findings
4. **Prioritize features** based on pain points
5. **Share findings** with development team
6. **Schedule follow-ups** if needed for clarification

**Success Criteria:** At least 2 interviews completed with detailed notes, clear pain points identified, and persona validation achieved.
